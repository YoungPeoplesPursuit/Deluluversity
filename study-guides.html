Everything You Need for the ACT

One of my most requested links to an Instagram Post: Ultimate ACT Tips

I got a 36 on both times I took the ACT. The first in sophomore year after 2 weeks of studying, the second during the state-mandated junior year ACT without studying. I'm not trying to flex, but rather to demonstrate that there are methodical ways to achieve your dream score. Honestly as long as you're in the high 30s you're good trying to improve from there has diminishing returns. If you really wanted to bump your 33 into a 36, understand those problems that trip you up.

When you understand that the ACT is trying to test content you should reasonably know and tests have similar problems, train to learn the problems and you're 85% there. The other 15% is honing pacing and strategy becuase in my opinion the biggest bottleneck is time. Especially a timesink, wasting lots of time on a problem that just ruins the rest of your performance.

Do practice tests. As many as you need to consistently hit your dream score:

  • The Official ACT website has a full-length test and practice problems
  • The big red ACT book is honestly all you need
  • When you make mistakes, analyze them, write your own solution, and know what problems you're likely to miss
  • I offer tutoring services! Contact me for more info
A more thorough article I wrote about ACT strategies

For those looking for SAT tips: I did not take the SAT, but I got a 1520 on the PSAT and my friends who have taken both find that ACT tips generally transfer well to the SAT.
